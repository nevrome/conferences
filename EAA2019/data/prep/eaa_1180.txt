SUGAR POTS IN THE LEVANT: MANY QUESTIONS, SOME ANSWERS
Author(s): Jones, Richard (University of Glasgow) - Grey, Anthony (Sandwich, Kent)
Presentation Format: Oral
This paper arises out of the study by Anthony Grey and the presenter of the sugar pots from the medieval (mainly 13th-14th
century) sugar installation at the Tawahin es-Sukkar situated near the modern town of Safi, at the southern end of the Dead Sea
in Jordan. Having briefly presented the morphological and material characteristics of these pots and compared them with their
counterparts from elsewhere in Jordan and Palestine, this paper will consider issues to do with the manufacture and distribution
