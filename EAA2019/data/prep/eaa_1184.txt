SWEET SALTY WATER: SUGAR MOULDS REMAINS FROM PORTUGUESE ARCHAEOLOGICAL CONTEXTS
(15TH - 18TH CENTURIES)
Author(s): Teixeira, Andre (NOVA FCSH) - Pinto Coelho, Inês - Ferreira, Sara (CHAM-NOVA FCSH)
Presentation Format: Oral
The sugar production was the great promoter of the Portuguese colonization in the Atlantic. The socio-economic model successfully used in the islands of Madeira and São Tomé e Príncipe, and later in Brazil, inspired other colonial processes in America.
