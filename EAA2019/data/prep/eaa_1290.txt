PSEUDO-REGIONALIZATION? REFLEXIONS OF SUPRAREGIONAL NETWORKS IN SOUTHERN GERMAN
POTTERY PRACTICES OF THE EARLY 3RD MILLENNIUM BC
Author(s): Gleich, Philipp (University of Basel)
Presentation Format: Oral
Until today Southern German Neolithic archaeology has been dominated by the culture historical approach, resting strongly on
stylistic pottery analysis. For the early 3rd Millennium BC scholars have defined multiple regional „culture groups“, often divided
by modern political borders, being suggestive of regionally distinct and separate stylistic developments.
