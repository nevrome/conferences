The main scope of this session is to offer an overview of how various European scholars approach the topic, while taking into
account that research in this field has produced quantitative and qualitative results that vary extensively across different European centers. It is for this particular reason that this session sets out to analyze the Late Roman and Early Middle Ages funerary
practices, which may provide us with precious insight into the multiculturalism of the ethnically diverse groups that came to
settle on the foundations of the former provinces of the Roman Empire from the Adriatic Sea and the Carpathians.
