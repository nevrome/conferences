ARCHAEOLOGY IN RUSSIA DURING THE COMMUNIST AND DEMOCRATIC PERIODS
Author(s): Engovatova, Asya (Institute of Archaeology Russian Academy of Sciences)
Presentation Format: Oral
Communist ideology, and claims that the country’s resources belong to the ‘people’, became the basis for protecting archaeological monuments as Soviet ‘national wealth’. The multinational USSR lacked a national ideology. All archaeological sites were seen
as indicators of stages of societal development, in line with Marxist theory. Studies of Stone Age sites – an era which ideology
claimed had no class divisions, just like the Medieval period – enjoyed great popularity.
