Lithic typology has been widely disparaged in recent decades in the study of the Upper Palaeolithic, as critiques concerning the
reliability and theoretical underpinnings of the approach have found broad acceptance. However, lithic typology remains a key
method for assemblage comparison, especially when comparing a large number of sites or carrying out preliminary analyses.
