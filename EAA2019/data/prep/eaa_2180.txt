ARCHAEOLOGICAL SIGNS OF ISLAMIC IDENTITY IN THE CITY OF BOLGAR
Author(s): Koval, Vladimir (Institute of Archaeology Russian Academy of Sciences)
Presentation Format: Oral
Religious identification markers are an important field of research in modern archeology. There are quite a lot of markers of Christian identity: these are crosses-pendants, that were worn both under and over clothing, temple buildings, signs of the cross
and special formulas of inscriptions on various objects. However, the Islamic symbol similar to the cross did not exist. Thanks
to written sources, there is now no doubt that the majority of the population of the city of Bolgar, one of the largest cities in the
state of Volga Bulgaria, which adopted Islam in 922, was Muslim. We list the main signs of Muslim identity, recorded in Bolgar
