The context is homogeneous and chronologically defined between the Augustan and the Tiberian age.
The presence of products from Cumae in many sites of Northern Europe, as well of the French and Spanish coast or Adriatic
areas, often stamped by producers known also in Cumae (Marii and Helvii) is an interesting clue to understand the circulation and
diffusion systems of local products in a international framework.
The contribution is focused on the local production of Cumae and an analysis of the circulation in other sites, with reference to
both productive, typological and finally chronological aspects.
